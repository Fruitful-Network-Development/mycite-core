**Scope**
This audit is grounded in the current repo plus live `fnd` state under `mycite-state`, especially [runtime_paths.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portal_core/shared/runtime_paths.py#L47), [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L172), [request_log_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/request_log_store.py#L31), [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L938), and the live contract/request-log files at [contract JSON](/srv/repo/mycite-core/mycite-state/instances/fnd/private/contracts/contract-3-2-3-17-77-1-6-4-1-4.3-2-3-17-77-2-6-3-1-6.json#L1) and [request_log.ndjson](/srv/repo/mycite-core/mycite-state/instances/fnd/private/network/request_log/request_log.ndjson#L1).

**A. Current-State Responsibility Map**
- [runtime_paths.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portal_core/shared/runtime_paths.py#L47) owns canonical storage layout; it implicitly owns migration policy too because it bakes in legacy request-log fallback paths; it is tightly coupled to request-log and contract persistence; that coupling looks intentional-but-transitional.
- [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L172) owns contract persistence and payload normalization; it implicitly owns compact-array revision fields, tracked-resource linkage, and some sync metadata shape that should not live in core contract storage; it is tightly coupled to contract APIs, handshake flows, inherited-resource sync, and datum resolution; that coupling is legacy/transitional.
- [contracts.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contracts.py#L331) owns CRUD endpoints for contracts; it implicitly owns sandbox compilation, MSS previewing, alias creation, and request-log side effects; it is tightly coupled to `SandboxEngine`, `request_log_store`, and aliases; that coupling looks accidental growth around a once-smaller API.
- [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L938) owns asymmetric proposal/confirmation flows; it also implicitly owns public contact-card discovery, signature verification, symmetric-key lifecycle, nonce tracking, renewal scheduling, and request-log emission; it is tightly coupled to contracts, vault keys, contact cards, and request logs; that coupling is intentional in behavior but architecturally over-concentrated.
- [request_log_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/request_log_store.py#L31) owns append/read for the canonical event stream; it implicitly owns protocol-envelope validation for some event shapes and type fanout; it is tightly coupled to runtime UI summaries and handshake/contract APIs; the append-only coupling is intentional, the protocol-shape role is accidental creep.
- [request_log_ui.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/request_log_ui.py#L81) and the live app in [app.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/runtime/flavors/fnd/app.py#L886) own request-log presentation; they implicitly treat request-log as a message-thread substrate and pseudo-channel inventory; they are tightly coupled to contract datum resolution; that coupling looks transitional and is the clearest sign that evidence and protocol surfaces are blending.
- [public_datum_resolver.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/public_datum_resolver.py#L103), [resolver.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/external_resources/resolver.py#L21), and [resource_registry.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/resource_registry.py#L316) own public-resource fetch, isolate formation, and local reference materialization; they implicitly rely on contracts only when callers choose contract snapshots or subscription flows; coupling to contracts is partial and mostly transitional.
- [inherited_contract_resources.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/inherited_contract_resources.py#L45) owns contract-linked resource subscriptions and refresh; it implicitly owns reference lineage scheduling and contract-side sync metadata, which should be separate; it is tightly coupled to contracts and resource registry; that coupling is legacy and directly conflicts with the target direction.
- [sandbox/models.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/sandbox/models.py#L1), [workspace_contract.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/sandbox/workspace_contract.py#L1), and [tool-layer-mediation.md](/srv/repo/mycite-core/wiki/tools/tool-layer-mediation.md#L11) show tool/sandbox mediation is mostly local-shell oriented, not network-contract oriented; the hidden coupling point is data publication APIs, not the tool layer itself.
- [shell_models.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/core_services/shell_models.py#L7), [profile_resolver.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/profile_resolver.py#L31), and the duplicate sanitizers/routes in [app.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/runtime/flavors/fnd/app.py#L1273) jointly own profile/contact-card publication; ownership is blurred and partly duplicated; that coupling looks accidental maintenance drift.

**B. Current Operational Model**
- Contracts currently work as durable JSON records keyed by portal pair, with MSS context, selected refs, status/type metadata, compact-index revision hints, and often tracked-resource linkage all normalized in [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L172). Handshake APIs then upsert those records during proposal, confirmation, and renewal in [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L1098).
- Foreign datum resolution through contracts is implemented in [mss/core.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/mss/core.py#L923): local refs resolve locally; foreign refs choose a contract by preferred ID or by first matching counterparty/owner MSN; then the resolver reads the current `counterparty_mss` or `owner_mss`. Current reality: this is contract-snapshot lookup, not a line-aware or version-aware resolution subsystem.
- `request_log` is a single append-only stream at `private/network/request_log/request_log.ndjson`, with legacy fallback reads in [runtime_paths.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portal_core/shared/runtime_paths.py#L55). The store validates only v1-like envelopes and accepts arbitrary other dict events in [request_log_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/request_log_store.py#L126). Current reality: it is evidence-first storage with weak schema boundaries.
- Resources and exposed resources are partly separate from contracts. Public contact-card resources are discovered and fetched via [resolver.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/external_resources/resolver.py#L21) without contract context, and local/inherited references are versioned via [resource_registry.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/resource_registry.py#L316). But contract-linked subscription refresh still routes through [inherited_contract_resources.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/inherited_contract_resources.py#L45).
- Tool mediation and sandbox mediation are largely independent of network contracts. The tool layer is defined as shell mediation in [tool-layer-mediation.md](/srv/repo/mycite-core/wiki/tools/tool-layer-mediation.md#L11), and sandbox contract structures are local workspace declarations in [workspace_contract.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/sandbox/workspace_contract.py#L1). The coupling point is that contract APIs sometimes call sandbox compilation and exposed-resource endpoints mix contact-card and sandbox publication.
- Profiles/contact-card resources are public-surface inputs to handshake and resource discovery. Handshake verification fetches contact cards and verifies signatures in [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L284), while the public profile routes are served from the live app in [app.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/runtime/flavors/fnd/app.py#L1273). Current reality: profile publication is already mostly outside contracts, but its code ownership is scattered.

**C. Boundary Audit**
- Communication line management is conflated with contract persistence in [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L425): symmetric session metadata is stored under `contract["symmetric"]`, while key material is in vault. That makes contracts act as both line identity and session-state container.
- Resource inventory/exposure is conflated with contracts in [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L185) and [inherited_contract_resources.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/inherited_contract_resources.py#L266): `tracked_resource_ids` and `inherited_resource_sync` make contracts behave like subscription registries.
- Foreign datum resolution is conflated with contract selection in [mss/core.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/mss/core.py#L954): contract choice falls back to “first matching msn,” so contract identity, communication line identity, and resource context identity are not cleanly separated.
- Compact-array context transport is conflated with contract revision protocol in [contracts.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contracts.py#L407) and [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L371): contract CRUD increments `compact_index_revision`, and compact-array update writes directly back into contract state.
- Revision/version tracking is partly separate in [resource_registry.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/resource_registry.py#L329) and partly embedded in contracts via `source_card_revision` / compact-index fields in [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L206). Current reality: contracts are carrying both context snapshot revision and resource-tracking hints.
- Event evidence vs protocol state is blurred because request-log is evidence in storage design, but the UI builds channels and P2P message views from it in [app.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/runtime/flavors/fnd/app.py#L903). Answer to focus question 2: it is not the full protocol state machine, but logic already treats it as operational conversation substrate.
- Local audit vs external audit is blurred in live data: the canonical request log contains local operational events like `alias.created` and service status/config events in [request_log.ndjson](/srv/repo/mycite-core/mycite-state/instances/fnd/private/network/request_log/request_log.ndjson#L1), despite the doc intent that cross-portal evidence be distinct.
- Profile/public exposure vs contract-mediated exchange is mostly separate, but hidden coupling exists where sandbox-exposed resources and contact-card public resources are bundled in one API in [data_workspace.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/data_workspace.py#L1432), and handshake depends on contact-card publication in [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L284).
- Answer to focus question 1: yes, contract logic is currently acting as compact-array context carrier, partial communication-line manager, revision tracker, request-log companion, and resource-link registry at once.
- Answer to focus question 3: exposed/public resources are not fully undermined by contracts, but hidden coupling through subscription tracking, mixed publication endpoints, and contract-snapshot datum resolution means the separation is incomplete.
- Answer to focus question 5: the strongest conflicting names/paths are `request_log` for what is drifting toward canonical system evidence, `/portal/api/inbox` as an alias over the same store, docs naming `private/request_log/<msn>.ndjson` vs live `private/network/request_log/request_log.ndjson`, and `ref.*` docs vs `rf.*` implementation.

**E. Separation Opportunities**
- `NetworkSession` or `CommunicationLine` service. Own proposal/confirmation, signature verification, symmetric renewal, nonce policy, due scheduling, and vault key references; move ownership out of [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L938); contracts should stop owning session-state shape; this reduces the biggest concentration of cross-cutting logic; do before further tool work.
- `ContractContext` store. Own peer IDs, contract status/type, selected refs, owner/counterparty MSS, and only the minimum line-policy metadata; move out compact-index revision protocol, tracked resources, and symmetric session fields from [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L172); this reduces accidental coupling between context exchange and other subsystems; do early, after session boundary is sketched.
- `CrossPortalEvidence` store. Own canonical append/read of external event evidence and typed fanout; [request_log_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/request_log_store.py#L31) should stop accepting unrelated local operational events; this would separate evidence from local audit and UI channel semantics; do first or second.
- `ResourcePublication` service. Own public contact-card resource descriptors and sandbox-exposed resource descriptors as distinct publication surfaces; [data_workspace.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/data_workspace.py#L1432) and [engine.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/sandbox/engine.py#L981) should stop co-presenting them as one blended shape; do before expanding public resource features.
- `ResourceLineage/Subscription` registry. Own tracked external-public-resource subscriptions, refresh state, version hashes, and disconnect status; move those concerns out of [inherited_contract_resources.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/inherited_contract_resources.py#L266) and contract payloads, and keep them next to [resource_registry.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/data_engine/resource_registry.py#L316); this most directly supports “resources remain independently accessible”; do before more lineage work.
- `ForeignDatumResolution` service. Own the resolution order and require an explicit line/contract identifier when multiple lines exist; [mss/core.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/mss/core.py#L923) should stop first-match fallback by MSN alone; do after contract/session boundaries are clarified.
- `ProfilePublication` service. Consolidate sanitization, path resolution, and route serialization now split between [shell_models.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/core_services/shell_models.py#L7), [profile_resolver.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/profile_resolver.py#L31), and [app.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/runtime/flavors/fnd/app.py#L1273); do after publication boundaries are cleaned up.

**F. Contract Destination Analysis**
- What already supports the target direction: the handshake flow already distinguishes asymmetric initiation from later symmetric operation in [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L1393), and contracts already hold the shared context pieces that fit a communication line in [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L172).
- What works against it: contracts currently absorb tracked-resource linkage, compact-array revision protocol, and some session-adjacent metadata, while request-log and UI logic blur evidence with messaging, and foreign datum resolution assumes contract snapshot equals resource-access path.
- What must split into a separate subsystem: resource lineage/version tracking; public/exposed resource publication; symmetric session/key lifecycle; cross-portal evidence taxonomy; explicit foreign datum resolution.
- What should remain contract-owned: portal pair identity, contract status/type, negotiated/shared context snapshots, selected refs, and line-level policy or pointers to active session state.
- What should move into `resource_lineage`: `tracked_resource_ids`, `inherited_resource_sync`, refresh bookkeeping, and reference materialization orchestration.
- What should move into `resource_publication`: contact-card public resource descriptors, exposed-resource descriptor emission, and publication schemas.
- What should move into `network_session`: asymmetric handshake records, symmetric key references, renewal/nonce state, endpoint advertisement, and channel lifecycle.
- Placement of symmetric-key channel management: adjacent to contract logic, not inside core contract storage. Target-aligned interpretation: the contract should own line identity and policy, while a session layer below/adjacent owns key negotiation, active session state, and crypto operational details.

**G. Concrete Recommended Refactor Order**
1. Priority: highest. Area: request-log/evidence boundary. Reason: current docs, naming, and live behavior diverge the most. Likely impact: high conceptual clarity, low code risk. Dependency: none.
2. Priority: high. Area: handshake/session extraction from [contract_handshake.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/api/contract_handshake.py#L938). Reason: this is the clearest seam toward “contracts as communication-line managers.” Likely impact: high. Dependency: evidence taxonomy clarified first.
3. Priority: high. Area: contract payload model cleanup. Reason: remove non-contract concerns from [contract_store.py](/srv/repo/mycite-core/portals/_shared/portal/services/contract_store.py#L172). Likely impact: high. Dependency: session boundary sketched.
4. Priority: high. Area: resource subscription/lineage split from contracts. Reason: current contract-linked subscriptions directly conflict with independently accessible resources. Likely impact: high. Dependency: contract payload cleanup.
5. Priority: medium-high. Area: foreign datum resolution. Reason: first-match-by-MSN will become unsafe once multiple lines/contracts coexist. Likely impact: medium-high. Dependency: explicit contract vs line identity cleanup.
6. Priority: medium. Area: public/exposed resource publication boundary. Reason: keeps public resource access independent from contract flows and sandbox internals. Likely impact: medium. Dependency: none beyond naming cleanup.
7. Priority: medium. Area: profile/contact-card publication consolidation. Reason: reduce duplication and schema drift around public JSON surfaces. Likely impact: medium. Dependency: publication boundary decision.
8. Priority: medium. Area: docs/path/naming reconciliation. Reason: after the above seams are agreed, update the glossary/wiki to reflect actual ownership and storage posture. Likely impact: medium. Dependency: prior boundary decisions.

Current reality: the repo already has the pieces needed for contracts to become communication-line managers, but contract storage, handshake/session logic, request-log evidence, and resource subscription/lineage are still partially collapsed into each other. Target-aligned interpretation: contracts should shrink toward line identity plus shared exchange context, while evidence, session state, public publication, and resource lineage become clearer neighboring subsystems.
